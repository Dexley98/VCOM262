By The Numbers...

Research

The International Anthropomorphic Research Project, a team of social scientists from various disciplines led by Plante, Reysen, Roberts, and Gerbasi, has been collecting data on the furry fandom using numerous methodologies. Their 2016 publication collects several peer-reviewed and self-published studies into a single volume. The data utilized was collected from furry conventions, in addition to, demographic data collected from furry forums and websites. These surveys encompass demographic, lifestyle, mental, and emotion based questions. The entire volume consits of a collective survey of about 15,000 people.

Sex and Gender identity

73% of furries are male.

27% of furries are female.

When asked, 67% of the fandom identify as masculine. 23% identify as feminine and 10% identify as genderqueer or non-binary.

In terms of sexuality and preferecnes, 23% claim to be heterosexual, 13% homosexual, and 64% as bisexual to some degree.

Ethnicity

83-90% of furries self-identify as White, with small minorities of furries self-identifying as Asian (2-4%), Black (2-3%), and Hispanic (3%).
